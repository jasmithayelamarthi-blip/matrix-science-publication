About Matrix

A student-run, peer-reviewed international journal giving rigorous high-school science a credible home.

Our mission

Matrix Science Publication exists to take young scientists seriously. We give high-school researchers a rigorous, transparent venue to publish real investigations — and we hold that work to standards modelled on professional journals.

Too much excellent secondary-school science is never shared beyond a classroom or a science fair. Matrix changes that. Every manuscript is read by student editors and external mentors, every claim must be supported by data, and every accepted paper is published openly so anyone, anywhere, can read it for free.

Our editorial process

Each submission travels through five stages before publication. The process is designed to be educational as well as selective — authors receive constructive feedback at every step.

  1. Submission & desk check

    Authors submit a manuscript with raw data and methods. An editor checks scope, originality, ethics and formatting, and confirms the work is the students’ own.

  2. Editorial assessment

    A subject editor evaluates the research question, methodology and significance, deciding whether the manuscript is ready to advance to review.

  3. Peer & mentor review

    Two reviewers — a trained student reviewer and a subject-expert mentor — assess validity, reproducibility and clarity, returning detailed comments.

  4. Revision

    Authors respond to every comment and resubmit. Editors verify that the science, data and writing now meet Matrix’s standards.

  5. Publication

    The accepted article is copy-edited, assigned to an issue and published open-access with its data — free to read forever.

Editorial values statement

Four commitments guide every decision we make.

🔍

Rigour

Conclusions must follow from the evidence. We publish honest results — including negative ones — over impressive-sounding claims.

🔓

Openness

Articles and their underlying data are free to read and reuse. Reproducibility starts with sharing your data and code.

⚖️

Integrity

We expect original work, honest authorship, and full disclosure of methods, assistance and limitations.

🌍

Access

Talent is everywhere; opportunity is not. Matrix welcomes authors from every country, school and background.

Editorial policies

  • Authorship & originality: submissions must be the authors’ own, unpublished work. Mentor and tool assistance must be disclosed.
  • Ethics & safety: any research involving human participants, animals or hazardous procedures must follow appropriate safety and consent guidelines.
  • Data availability: raw data and analysis code should accompany every empirical submission.
  • Corrections: errors discovered after publication are corrected transparently with a dated note.
  • Conflicts of interest: editors recuse themselves from handling work by close collaborators or schoolmates.

History of Matrix

Matrix Science Publication was founded by high-school researcher Jasmitha out of a simple frustration: brilliant student science had nowhere credible to live. What began as a single editor and a shared document has grown into an international, multi-subject journal with a structured peer-review process and an open editorial board.

The name Matrix reflects our belief that science is connected — that physics, biology, chemistry, astrophysics and biotechnology are cells in one larger grid of knowledge, each strengthening the others.

Long-term vision

We are building the journal we wish had existed when we started. Our long-term goals:

  • Issue citable identifiers so student work can be referenced like any scholarly article.
  • Pair every author with a mentor reviewer, turning publication into a genuine learning experience.
  • Grow regional editorial chapters so reviewing reflects a truly global community.
  • Maintain a permanent, free, open archive of high-school science for the public good.
